SAM CORDINGLEY

Heatmap News is a website that displays the most important world news stories on a map of the Earth. It lets you quickly see what is happening in the world right now. The front-end was developed with HTML/CSS/JavaScript/jQuery while the back-end was developed with Python/Django. The website is no longer active but received press coverage here.

I wrote my MEng dissertation on "Light Field Rendering for Real-time Applications" and developed a system for capturing light fields of Autodesk Maya scenes with Pixar's RenderMan and rendering them in real-time within Unreal Engine 4. Read the dissertation here.

I developed a rasterisation graphics engine using C++. It's features include shadows and bump mapping.

I developed a raytracer graphics engine using C++. It's features include textures, shadows, and reflections.

I designed and created a complex 3D model of a robot. The model was created using Autodesk Maya.

I created an animation of a dancing robot using Autodesk Maya. You can watch the videos here and here.

I spent a year as the lead programmer of a group of seven developing a multiplayer virtual reality game for the Oculus Rift DK2 called Tomb of Terror. The game worked around an echolocation mechanic where sound was visualised as glowing waves propagating throughout the room. I built the echolocation mechanic using a combination of C# and GLSL within the Unity game engine. Click here and here for videos demonstrating the sound vision shader.

Worked with the Environment Agency to replace their 'What's in your backyard' service with a modern cross-platform mobile app. Specifically, I worked on the back-end system, providing environmental data for the app. I also helped implement Android specific features on the front-end. (http://samcordingley.com/ea/)

I developed a game in 48 hours for the Ludum Dare game jam competition. The game is a platformer in which you control a box which freezes after 10 seconds. After the box has frozen a new box spawns, this allows you to progress through the level by positioning your boxes before the 10 seconds runs out. Download the game here.
